Facilities

Our company offers state-of-the-art and easily accessible facilities. We are the first Vehicle Technical Inspection Stations (EKLAV) in Greece to feature 6 automatic inspection lines. Additional advantages include modern and welcoming waiting areas, specifically designed to accommodate vehicle owners during the inspection process, thereby ensuring a comfortable, safe, and above all, dignified stay.

Certifications and Services

Our company’s model EKLAV stations are certified under very strict conditions and criteria by the Ministry of Infrastructure and Transport (YME) and the Hellenic Accreditation System (ESYD). We operate as an independent certified vehicle technical inspection body and issue official KTEO technical inspection reports and emissions cards. At the same time, we offer drivers the option to carry out a voluntary – preventive technical inspection in cases such as before a long trip, when buying or selling a vehicle, or before/after vehicle repairs or servicing, providing them with a certificate of proper and safe operation.

Equipment

All our stations are equipped with state-of-the-art technology from world-leading companies. Our model EKLAVY stations are certified under very strict conditions and criteria by the Ministry of Infrastructure and Transport (YME) and the Hellenic Accreditation System (ESYD).

Maintenance Instructions

5 Simple Checks You Can Do Yourself

The most important thing you can do to reduce the risk of your car breaking down is to service it according to the schedule and specifications of the manufacturer’s manual. In addition to regular servicing, follow the tips below to enjoy driving without worries or fear.


1. TIRES

Check the correct pressure your tires should have and inspect it at least every 15 days using a high-quality gauge or workshop air gauge. Examine the tread and condition of the tires; look for cuts on the sidewalls. If any tire needs regular inflation, it might have a small puncture. Don’t forget to check the spare tire as well.

2. ENGINE OIL

Check the oil level every 15 days and before each trip. Change the oil and filter regularly according to the manufacturer’s specifications using the correct type of oil. Excessive oil consumption indicates a possible engine issue.

3. BODYWORK

If there is damage to the car body, repair it immediately to prevent rust. Many cars have a bodywork warranty, which requires annual inspection at the dealership. Check whether the previous owner kept a file with the vehicle’s inspection history.

4. COOLANT (WATER)

Check the coolant level regularly and top it up as needed, when the engine is cold. Before winter, check the antifreeze concentration. Antifreeze prevents corrosion in the cooling system and stops the coolant from freezing, so it is essential year-round.

5. TOOLKIT

Make sure you know where the basic car toolkit is located. It should include at least a jack and tools for removing the wheels. Learn the car’s jacking points for safe lifting. Also, ensure the toolkit contains a wrench or other tool for loosening the wheel bolts.

Operating Rules


Reinspection and Rules:

Your vehicle must be registered in the National Unified Vehicle Database and be insured. If it is not insured, a fine of €200 is imposed, and the customer is required to insure it and return.

If serious deficiencies are found in your vehicle and repairs are needed, you have 30 days to return for a reinspection and issuance of the technical inspection certificate. The technical director may grant an additional 10 days in cases where repairs are extensive.

If the customer returns after the deadline, a fine equal to 50% of the regular inspection fee is charged (see the table below).
